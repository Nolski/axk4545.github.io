Chicago Landmarks

The Chicago Water Tower

Built in 1869, the 154 foot tower is one of only a few buildings in the area to survive the Chicago Fire in 1871.

Navy Pier

Built in 1915 as a terminal for passenger and freight traffic on Lake Michigan and public events, the pier was used as a training facility by the United States Navy during World War II. The pier was converted into it's current state as a mix of shopping, dining, entertainment and outdoor space. The most prominent feature of the pier is the ferris wheel. The current one, installed in May 2016, is 196 feet tall.

Cloud Gate

Known commonly as The Bean for its kidney bean like shape, this sculpture was designed by British artist Anish Kapoor.The sculpture is designed to reflect a distorted image of the Chicago skyline.

Sears Tower

The Sears Tower was built in 1973 as the Chicago headquarters of retailer Sears, Roebuck and Company. Sears wished to consolidate their Chicago employees into the 108 story structure. On a clear day, you can see across Lake Michigan to Indiana, Michigan and Wisconsin from the skydeck observation deck on the 103rd floor.

Buckingham Fountain

Buckingham Fountain was donated to the city by Kate Buckingham as a memorial to her brother Clarence. It was dedicated on August 26th, 1927. Both Kate and Clarence were patrons of the arts so it is a fitting memorial. The central jet can shoot water 150 feet into the air.
